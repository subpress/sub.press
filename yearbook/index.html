About the yearbook

Now in it's 8th year, the Yearbook is a time-capsule of sorts, celebrating the global exploring community's creativity, talent, and insanity. It captures who was around, and what we did.

The content is a solid mix of behind the scenes action shots and pristinely laid out tripod photography. Photos depicting time and place are most likely to make the cut.

The Yearbook's contributors and audience are personally vetted, and is not open to the public. Please don't share submission details or copies of the book without asking first.

Photo requirements

  • Taken between 1st of Jan 2024 and 31st Dec 2024
  • Visibly exploring-related
  • People in frame
  • Photos you have personally taken
  • Phone / DSLR / Film photos are all fine
  • 1000px minimum on the largest side
  • Faces uncensored
  • Maximum 100 images per contributor

Notes

Faces are blurred on request, but I like to make them all the same style. It is your responsibility to ask people in your photos for consent to be in the Yearbook.

Images that accompany a story do not need to include people.

If the thought of editing all your photos is daunting, submit them with a note and I'll request the finals of those that make the cut.

Story requirements

  • Max 1,500 words
  • No names or location details if it's spicy
  • Up to 2 stories per contributor
  • At least one image per story
  • Submitted as a file, eg. Google Doc / Word / Notepad

Notes

If you've got a story to tell but aren't a confident writer, get in touch and we can workshop something.

Images that accompany a story do not need to include people.
